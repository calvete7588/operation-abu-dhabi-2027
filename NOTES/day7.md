## Objective
Analyze the CISSP security domains, understand the deep/dark web structures, dissect ransomware threats, and master the 7 steps of the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF).

## Core Concepts Explained
* **CISSP 8 Domains:** Universal operational divisions of security tasks (e.g., Asset Security, Identity and Access Management, Security Operations).
* **Ransomware & Malware:** Malicious software designed to block access to a computer system until a sum of money is paid.
* **The 3 Layers of the Web:**
  * *Surface Web:* Publicly indexed pages.
  * *Deep Web:* Pages behind paywalls/logins (databases, private emails).
  * *Dark Web:* Encrypted networks hosting anonymous and often illicit activities.
* **NIST RMF (7 Steps):** A structured process (Prepare, Categorize, Select, Implement, Assess, Authorize, Monitor) used to manage security and privacy risks.

## Technical Vocabulary
* **Ransomware** (/rán-som-uer/): Secuestro de datos mediante cifrado.
* **Deep Web** (/diip ueb/): Web profunda (no indexada).
* **RMF** (/ar-em-ef/): Risk Management Framework (Marco de Gestión de Riesgos).
